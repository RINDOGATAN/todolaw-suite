# DPO Central — Demo Video Script

**Duration**: ~4 minutes
**Tone**: Professional but approachable. Educational without being patronizing. Think "calm product walkthrough with privacy context woven in."
**Audience**: DPOs, privacy officers, business owners evaluating compliance tools.
**Narrator**: Calm, confident voice. Not rushed.

---

## SCENE 1 — Hook (0:00–0:15)

**On screen**: Split view — left: a stressed person surrounded by spreadsheets. Right: clean DPO Central dashboard.

**Narration**:
> Managing GDPR compliance across dozens of vendors, data flows, and subject requests shouldn't mean living in spreadsheets. DPO Central gives you a structured privacy program in minutes — not months.

---

## SCENE 2 — Sign In & First Impression (0:15–0:35)

**On screen**: Sign-in page. User types email, clicks "Continue with Email." Cut to the magic link confirmation. Cut to dashboard loading.

**Narration**:
> Getting started is simple. Sign in with your email or Google account — no long onboarding forms. You land on your privacy dashboard: a single view of your data inventory, open DSARs, assessments, and incidents.

**Key UI to show**:
- Sign-in card with email input and Google button
- Dashboard with 4 stat cards (Data Inventory, Open DSARs, Assessments, Incidents)
- Quick Actions panel

---

## SCENE 3 — Quickstart Wizard (0:35–1:30)

**On screen**: User clicks "Get Started with Quickstart" card on dashboard. Wizard opens.

**Narration**:
> Most tools expect you to build your privacy program from scratch. DPO Central's Quickstart Wizard bootstraps it for you. You have two paths — use them together or separately.

### 3a — Vendor Import (0:45–1:05)

**On screen**: User selects "Import from Vendor Catalog." Search bar appears. User types "Slack", selects it. Types "Notion", selects it. Types "Stripe", selects it. Three vendor badges appear above the search.

**Narration**:
> First, tell us which vendors you use. Search our starter catalog of common vendors. Select the ones your organization relies on — Slack, Notion, Stripe — and DPO Central already knows what data they process, where they store it, and what transfer safeguards apply.

**Key UI to show**:
- "5 Free" badge on the vendor card (no premium needed)
- Vendor search dropdown with GDPR/Verified badges
- Selected vendor chips accumulating

### 3b — Industry Template (1:05–1:15)

**On screen**: User also selects "Start from Industry Template." Grid of industry cards appears. User clicks "E-Commerce / Retail."

**Narration**:
> Optionally, pick your industry. The e-commerce template adds common data assets like your customer database, order processing system, and marketing platform — with all the data elements and processing activities pre-mapped.

### 3c — Review & Execute (1:15–1:30)

**On screen**: Review step. Summary stat cards show: 3 Vendors, 9 Data Assets, 47 Data Elements, 9 Activities, 2 Transfers, 1 AI System. The blue "Non-destructive" notice is visible. User clicks "Build My Privacy Program." Spinner, then success screen with green checkmark.

**Narration**:
> Review what will be created — vendors, data assets, data elements, processing activities, cross-border transfers. Nothing existing gets modified, duplicates are skipped. One click, and your privacy program is live.

**Key UI to show**:
- Bot icon next to an AI-capable vendor (e.g., if one was selected)
- "1 AI System" stat card
- Success screen with navigation cards

---

## SCENE 4 — Data Inventory & ROPA (1:30–2:00)

**On screen**: User clicks "Data Inventory" from success screen. Asset list loads with the auto-generated assets.

**Narration**:
> Your data inventory is now populated. Each vendor import created a data asset with its data elements — names, emails, payment details — categorized by sensitivity. Processing activities are linked to their legal basis, data subjects, and retention periods. This is your Record of Processing Activities — your ROPA — generated automatically.

**Key UI to show**:
- Asset list with type badges (SaaS Application, etc.)
- Click into one asset: data elements table with category, sensitivity, retention
- Processing Activities tab: activity linked to asset with legal basis shown

---

## SCENE 5 — DSAR Management (2:00–2:30)

**On screen**: Switch to DSAR module. Show the list view, then cut to the public portal.

**Narration**:
> When a data subject wants to exercise their rights, they use your public DSAR portal — a branded, no-login-required form where they select their request type: access, erasure, rectification, portability. They submit it, get a reference number, and can track its status.

**On screen**: Cut back to internal DSAR queue showing the new request with SLA countdown.

**Narration**:
> Internally, you see every request with SLA tracking. GDPR gives you 30 days — DPO Central counts down for you and flags overdue requests. Assign tasks to team members, communicate with the requester, and close it out — all in one place.

**Key UI to show**:
- Public portal form with request type dropdown
- Success screen with reference number (DSR-ABC12345)
- Internal queue with SLA badges (green "12 days", red "Overdue")

---

## SCENE 6 — AI Governance Register (2:30–3:05)

**On screen**: Navigate to AI Systems in the sidebar. Show the list with risk level badges and stats.

**Narration**:
> The EU AI Act takes effect in August 2026. If your organization uses AI, you need to know your risk exposure now. DPO Central's AI Governance Register classifies every AI system by the Act's four risk levels — from minimal to unacceptable.

**On screen**: Click into a system detail page. Show the risk level badge, capabilities, EU AI Act role, compliance fields.

**Narration**:
> When you imported vendors during quickstart, DPO Central automatically detected AI-capable vendors and created AI system records — pre-filled with capabilities, techniques, and an initial risk classification based on the catalog data.

**On screen**: Show the AI Sentinel integration card with "Linked to AI Sentinel" and the deep link button.

**Narration**:
> For organizations that need deeper AI governance — risk history, compliance frameworks, oversight gates — DPO Central connects to AI Sentinel. Export your register with one click, and navigate between apps seamlessly.

**Key UI to show**:
- Risk stats: High Risk count in orange, Minimal in green
- System detail: AI Capabilities badges, EU AI Act Role, ISO 42001 badge
- Blue "Linked to AI Sentinel" card with "Open in AI Sentinel" button
- "Send to AI Sentinel" export dialog on list page

---

## SCENE 7 — Vendor Management & Expert Help (3:05–3:30)

**On screen**: Navigate to Vendors. Show the list with risk tier badges.

**Narration**:
> Every vendor is tracked with their risk tier, certifications, contract status, and data processing details. Need a deeper assessment? DPO Central connects you with privacy experts — DPO-as-a-service providers, DPIA specialists, and legal advisors — filtered by specialization, country, and language.

**On screen**: Show the Expert Help CTA. Click through to the expert directory. Show filters and expert cards.

**Key UI to show**:
- Vendor cards with CRITICAL/HIGH/LOW risk badges
- Expert directory with specialization and country filters
- Expert profile cards

---

## SCENE 8 — Closing (3:30–3:55)

**On screen**: Return to dashboard. Zoom out slightly to show the full layout.

**Narration**:
> DPO Central gives you a complete privacy program — data inventory, DSAR management, impact assessments, incident tracking, vendor oversight, and AI governance — all from a single dashboard. Start with the free tier, add the vendor catalog and premium assessments when you need them.

**On screen**: Fade to centered logo + tagline.

> Your privacy program, structured and running, in under five minutes.

**On screen**: URL and "Get Started Free" CTA.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Screen Recording Checklist
- Use the demo org (`demo` slug, `demo@privacysuite.example`)
- Seed demo data first: `npx prisma db seed`
- Browser: Chrome, 1920x1080, clean profile, dark mode off
- Hide bookmarks bar and extensions
- Use a slow, deliberate mouse — no frantic clicking
- Each scene should be a continuous recording, edited together

### Data to Pre-Seed
- 3–5 vendors (mix of risk tiers: Stripe=LOW, OpenAI=HIGH, Slack=LOW)
- 1 open DSAR with SLA countdown
- 1 AI system linked to AI Sentinel (set `aiSentinelSystemId` manually)
- 1 high-risk AI system (recruitment screening) for contrast
- 1 incident (closed, for the stats)

### Transitions
- Scene-to-scene: Quick fade (0.3s) or direct cut
- Within scenes: Follow the mouse naturally, no zooming unless highlighting a small element
- Callout boxes: Use subtle rounded rectangles to highlight key UI elements when narration references them

### Music
- Light, ambient background — not corporate stock music
- Lower volume during narration, slightly higher during transitions
- No music during the quickstart execution moment (let the UI speak)
